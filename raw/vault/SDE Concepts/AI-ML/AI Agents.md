# AI Agents

YT Video: **What are AI Agents? by IBM Technology**

 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F8NKVhkZZWI

![[Screenshot_2025-04-01_at_16.08.05.png]]

The video discusses the evolution from monolithic AI models to compound AI systems and further into AI Agents.

**Monolithic Models:** These models are limited by their training data, making them difficult to adapt and apply to tasks requiring external knowledge.

**Compound AI Systems:** These systems integrate AI models into existing processes, allowing them to access relevant data and solve problems more effectively. They are modular, combining various AI models and programmatic components. A key example is answering the question, "How many vacation days do I have left?" by accessing a database with that information. Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) is mentioned as a common type of compound AI system.

**AI Agents:** These systems use Large Language Models (LLMs) to control the logic of compound AI systems. They can break down complex problems and create a plan to solve them. Key capabilities of LLM Agents include:

- **Reasoning:** Creating a plan to solve a problem.
- **Acting:** Using external programs or "tools" (like search engines, databases, or other models) to execute the plan.
- **Memory:** Storing inner logs and conversation history for personalization.

**React Agents:** One popular way to configure AI agents is through React, which combines reasoning and acting. An example is provided of a React agent helping someone plan a vacation to Florida by using tools to determine how many bottles of sunscreen to bring based on vacation length, weather forecasts, and recommended dosage.

The video concludes by stating that compound AI systems are becoming more agentic, with the level of LLM autonomy adjustable based on the task's complexity. Programmatic approaches may be more efficient for narrow problems, while agentic approaches are better for complex tasks. The field of agentic systems is rapidly progressing.
